New approaches to pest management Making agriculture more environmentally friendly Integrated pest management (IPM)Shift in agriculture from conventional chemical pesticides to more sustainable and eco-friendly approaches to controlling pests, weeds, and diseases. To manage pest damage in the most economical way that minimises damage to humans, other organisms, and the environment. The IPM approach is not confined solely to agriculture with relevant applications to gardening and land management. The Four Pillars of IPM * Identify and monitor o Aim: * To recognise quickly when there is a pest problem, so that control measures can be used only, when necessary, rather than pre-emptively o Methods: * Inspection of crops * Pest, weed, and disease identification * Forecasting and assessing levels of pest populations and diseases * Set action thresholds o Aim: * To ensure that control measures are only used, when necessary, thereby limiting excessive use of measureso Methods: * A threshold for crop damage is set, above which further pest damage is economically important * If this threshold is surpassed, then control action is taken * Prevention o Aim: * To prevent pests from becoming a significant problem o Methods: * Are often cultural Đ focused on the crops grown and methods used to do so* Crop rotation * Cultivation and tillage practices* Growing pest and disease-resistant varieties * Using trap crops to draw away pests* Hygiene measures Đ regular cleansing of machinery and equipment  * Specific to WOSR (Our problem)* Planting WOSR at a time such that the time young seedlings appear doesnŐt coincide with adult CSFB migration, thereby reducing the damage caused by CSFB feeding o Early planting (late July Đ early August)*  Allows time for crop establishment and development before the peak activity of adult beetles, such that WOSR crops are more resilient against feeding damage o Late planting (September onwards)* Allows WOSR to avoid the peak migration of adult CSFB, which reduces the adult feeding pressure during earlier WOSR crops establishment and development. However, the late season conditions are likely to be to colder and wetter, which are unfavourable for WOSR crop establishment* Control o Aim: * Effective, eco-friendly methods to target pest populations, when preventative actions are no longer effective* To use pest-specific methods that minimise off-target effects on other organisms such as pollinators and the environmento Methods: * Biological * Introduce natural predators* Using natural parasites or pathogens to control pest populations o EPNo EPF * Physical / mechanical * NMW - Traps, barriers, mowing or hand-pulling to physically remove pests * Cultural * NMW - Modifying the environment to make it less favourable for pests (soil management, strategic planting times)* Chemical pesticides * Using the minimal effective dose and application frequency * Targeted application using precision technology such as spot treatments * Biopesticides ** definition of biopesticide - Pesticides derived from natural materials that control pests through natural processesAdvantages of biopesticides * Highly specific with limited off-target effects on pollinators and humans * Biodegradable which reduces persistence and environmental impacts ** The development of insect specific biopesticides is a rapidly progressing field due to the incorporation of AI protein design technologies into pest science.  // examples of of new biopesticides: single domain antibodies ..
